A growing body of work on reinforcement learning has demonstrated the effectiveness of initializing policies with demonstrations prior to task-specific optimization. Rather than viewing such warm-start strategies solely as useful for accelerating convergence, we frame them as a generalizable learning paradigm. From this perspective, behavior cloning-base warm up is not just convenient for sample efficiency, but a tool that stabilizes early learning dynamics and constrains optimization to behaviorally plausible regions of policy space. Building on this insight, our proposal focuses on leveraging warm-start methods across different tasks and embodiments. We evaluated two modifications in four different MuJoCo tasks in the Gymnasium. The first modification follows the standard approach of pretraining in behavioral cloning (BC) followed by additional fine-tuning of reinforcement learning (RL). The second adopts a posterior behavior cloning initialization, which more explicitly preserves the structure induced by the demonstration data during subsequent RL optimization. Beyond in-domain learning, we further propose investigating the transferability of these warm-start methods under embodiment changes. After training policies in the original environment, we will modify the morphology of the agents (e.g., altered limb configurations) and evaluate how efficiently the pretraining policies adapt via RL fine-tuning. This setting allows us to study how different warm-start strategies influence transfer learning speed and robustness when faces with distribution shifts.
